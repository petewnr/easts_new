Junior Development Squad

training

The Junior Development Squad is run for those players from 9 – 15 years of age. Split into two section based on age and skills level.

The junior group (ages 9 – 12 years) work on a modified program with smaller courts and lower net heights. The drills focus on the raw motor skills of moving and eye-hand coordination to play the base skills of the game of volleyball.

The older group (ages 13-15 years) work at developing and mastering their technique and base skills to reach their full potential, and at the same time learn the importance of teamwork. Skills they can take back to their school, regional and state teams.

Players from this squad have the opportunity to trial and represent Easts in both of Brisbane’s major Volleyball Leagues with many going on to represent their Brisbane regional teams and State teams.

These young players will be coached by our team of highly experienced coaches, some of them with more than 20 years of coaching.

focus of training

  • Developing solid base skills
  • Strategies for game play
  • Team building and development of teamwork skills
  • Fitness - strength, speed and agility

    competition (as proposed from 2019)

    The competition runs for six rounds plus two weeks of finals. The season runs from mid-May to early August. The weeks are not consecutive weeks and are chosen to avoid school holidays and major school tournaments that happen during this time. Therefore, on the day of each round the teams play two games and have a duty.

    Tournaments

    Our junior teams are able to participate in a number of tournaments throughout the year. The tournaments easts usually enter teams are: Dragons Flaming Chalice – held each year over the Labour day long weekend in May. Chalice is one of the premier tournaments in Australia and attracts over 90 teams each year. This tournament has divisions for players of all skills.

    VQ State Championships – is held in Brisbane every year in August. Easts usually sends a selection of players from the PVL teams. This tournament is best suited to competitive to elite players.
